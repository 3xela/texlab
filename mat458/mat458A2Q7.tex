\begin{document}\noindent 5.5.56: Note that the smallest closed subspace that contains $E$ is by definition $ \ol{span(E)}$.
We claim that $ \ol{span(E)} = E^{\perp \perp}$. If $v \in E^{\perp \perp}$ then for all $u \in E^\perp$, $\inn{v,u} = 0$. Therefore $v \in span(E)$ and so it belongs to the closure. 
Now suppose that $v\in span(E)$. Let $\{v_n\}$ be a sequence in $span(E)$ converging to $v$ in the closure. We have that for all $u\in E^\perp$, $\inn{v_n, u} = 0$. Since the inner product is continuous, we have that $\inn{v,u}=0$. Thus $v\in E^{\perp \perp}$.
