Since $f,g$ are both integrable, define $h = g-f$. By our results from question 1, $h$ is integrable, with $h\geq 0$. Thus for any partition $P$, both $L(h,P)\geq 0$ and $U(h,P)\geq 0$. Therefore, $0\leq L(h)= U(h) = \int_A h$. We see that 
\begin{align*}
    & 0\leq \int_A h
    \\ & \implies 0 \leq \int_A (g-f)
    \\ & \implies \int_A f \leq \int_A g \tag*{by question 1}
\end{align*} As desired.
