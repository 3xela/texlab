Q4:\newline
Suppose that $\inn{x,y}=0$. It follows that 
\begin{align*}
    & \norm{x+y}^2
    \\ & = \inn{x+y,x+y}
    \\ & = \inn{x,x} + 2 \inn{x,y} + \inn{y,y}
    \\ & = \inn{x,x} + \inn{y,y}
    \\ & = \norm{x}^2 + \norm{y}^2 \quad    \qed
\end{align*}
