\noindent Consider the $k+l$ form $\omega \wedge \eta$. By Stoke's Theorem, we have that 
$$\int_M d(\omega \wedge \eta) = \int_{\bd M} \omega \wedge \eta = \int_{\emptyset} \omega \wedge \eta=0$$ Using the properties of the exteriour derivative, we know that $$d(\omega \wedge \eta) = d\omega \wedge \eta + (-1)^{k}\omega \wedge d\eta$$
Therefore $$ 0 = \int_{M} d\omega \wedge \eta + (-1)^k \omega \wedge d\eta \implies (-1)^{k+1} \int_{M} d\omega \wedge \eta = \int_{M}\omega \wedge d\eta$$
