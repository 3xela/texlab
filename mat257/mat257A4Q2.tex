Define a function $g$ as $$g(y) = f(1,y)$$ Notice that from the definiton of $g$ $$\frac{dg}{dy} = D_2f(1,y)$$
Now we have that $$g(y) = 1^{1^{1^{1{^y}}}} + log(1)arctan(arctan(arctan(sin(cos(y)-log(1+y))))) = 1$$ since $log(1)=0$ and $1^n=1$ for all $n$. Therefore from basic derivatives in 1 dimension we have that $\frac{dg}{dy} =0 = D_2f(1,y)$
