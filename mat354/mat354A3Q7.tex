\noindent Q7: We have that if $$w = z- \sqrt{z^2-1}$$ Therefore, $$w-z = -\sqrt{z^2-1} \implies w^2 -2zw +1=0. $$
So we consider the space $X = \{(z,w): w^2-2zw +1=0 \} \subset \C^2$. This takes on multiple values on the interval $[-1,1]$. Hence we can construct a Riemann surface on which $w$ is single valued by taking $2$ copies of $\C$ and identifying them on the interval $[-1,1]$. We identify the point $-1$ with $1$ and vice versa. This will ensure that $w$ lifts to a single valued function.
