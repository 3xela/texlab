\noindent Q6: Consider the function $f(x) = \begin{cases} 6x(1-x) & x\in[0,1] \\ 0 & x \notin [0,1]  \end{cases}$ Define $f_n(x) = nf(nx)$. We have that $f_n(x)=0$ for $nx\geq 1$, therefore $\lim_{n\to \infty}f_n(x)=0$. However, $$\int_0^1 f_n(x) dx = \int_0^{\infty} f_n(x) = \int_{0}^{\infty} nf(nx) dx = \int_{0}^{\infty}f(y) dy =1$$
And therefore, $\lim_{n\to \infty}\int_0^1f_n(x) = 1$
