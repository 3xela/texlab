\noindent Q3: Let $\varepsilon>0$. There exists some $\delta >0$ such that $|x-y|<\delta$ implies $|f_n(x) - f_n(y)|<\varepsilon$. We write that $|f(x)-f(y)| = |f_n(\frac{x}{n}) - f_n(\frac{y}{n})|<\varepsilon$ 
Therefore, by continuity of $f_n$, $|\frac{x}{n}-\frac{y}{n}|= \frac{1}{n}|x-y|<\delta$. Therefore, $|f(x)-f(y)|<\varepsilon$ for all $\varepsilon>0$ and sufficiently large n. Therefore, $f(x)=f(y)$, and we conclude that $f$ is a constant function.
