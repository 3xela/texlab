\noindent Q3: Suppose that there were a countable dense subset of $l^\infty$. Let $\{ \{ x_n \}^k \}_{k\in \N}$ be an enumeration of the dense subset. Define the sequence $\{y_n\}$ by $$y_i = \begin{cases} x_i^i +1 \text{ if $|x^i_i | \leq 1$} \\ 0 \text{ if $|x_i^i| >1$}\end{cases}$$
We have that $\{y_n\} \in l^\infty$ since each point in the sequence is either $0$ or less than or equal to 2. By construction we have that $|y_i - x^k_i| \geq 1$ for all $i,k$. Hence $\sup_n |y_n - x_n^k | \geq 1$.
