Q3: The claim is false. Consider $G = S_4$, $K = A_4$. It is known that $A_4$ is normal in $S_4$ since it is the kernel of the homeomorphism $sgn: S_4 \to \{-1,1\}$. Consider the subgroup $H = \{e, (12)(34)\}$. It is clear that $H$ is a subgroup and $H < K$. 
Consider the permutation $\sigma = (23)\in S_4$. We compute that $$\sigma H = \{\sigma, (1243)\}$$ but on the other hand $$H \sigma = \{\sigma,(1342) \}$$
We have found $H < K \triangleleft G$ with $H$ not normal in $G$. The claim is false.
