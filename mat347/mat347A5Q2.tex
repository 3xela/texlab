\noindent Q2: The normal subgroups of $S_4$ are $S_4$, $A_4$, $\{e\}$ and $\{e,(12)(34), (13)(24), (14)(23) \}$. We claim that there are no other normal subgroups of $S_4$. We know from the class equation that $$24 = |Z(G)|  + \sum_{g_i \in G} [G: C(g_i)] = 1 + 3+6+6+ 8$$
We cant have a subgroup of order 12 not equal to $A_4$, since theres no way to get a divisor of 24 without adding $8,3,1$, which already correspond to $A_4$. The only other way to get a divisor of $24$, is to have $3+1=4$, which corresponds to the subgroup $\{e,(12)(34), (13)(24), (14)(23) \}$.
