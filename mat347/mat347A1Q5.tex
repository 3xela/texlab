Q5: We will identify all the subgroups of $\Qu$: 
\begin{align*}
    \inn{1} & = \{1 \} 
    \\ \inn{- 1} & = \{ \pm 1\}
    \\ \inn{i} &= \{1,i,-1,-i \}
    \\ \inn{j} & = \{ 1,j,-1,-j\} 
    \\ \inn{ k} & = \{1,k,-1,-k \}
    \\ \inn{i,j} & = \{1,i,j,-1,-i,-j,k,-k \} = \Qu
\end{align*}
There are no other subgroups of $\Qu$, since if we took any 2 of $\{i,j,k \}$ we could generate the entire group.
