Q4: If $H$ is a $p-$subgroup that contains $N$, then the fourth isomorphism theorem tells us that $H/N$ will be a $p-$group of $G/N$. If $H\leq N$ is a $p-$group of $G$, then every element of $H/N$ will be sent to $eN$ by the projection mapping. Hence there can be at most $n_p(G)$ p groups in $G/N$. Therefore $n_p(G/N) \leq n_p(G)$. If $N = \{e\}$, then we have that $n_p(G) = n_p(G/N)$, so the inequality may not be strict.
