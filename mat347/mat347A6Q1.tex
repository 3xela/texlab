Consider the Heisenberg group $H(\F_p) = \Bigg\{ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & a & b \\ 0 & 1 & c \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}: a,b,c\in \F_p \Bigg\}$ for $p$ prime. This is a group, and has an order of $|G| = p^3$. Note that this is not abelian, since matrices only commute with diagonal matrices.
