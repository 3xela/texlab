Q3: It is clear that $|\Z / p\Z|=p$. Let $n\in C_p$ be a nonidentity element. We claim that $\inn{n} = C_p$. By Lagrange's Theorem, the size of any subgroup of $C_p$ must divide $p$. Since $p$ is prime we have that every subgroup must either be trivial or the entire group. Since $\inn{n}$ is not trivial, since we assume $n\neq e$, we have that $\inn{n}= C_p$.
