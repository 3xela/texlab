\begin{document} \noindent Q5: Let $H \leq G$ ,$|G| = p^\alpha m$ and $|H| = p^\beta n $. By Sylow's Theorem, there is a $Q \leq H$,which is a Sylow $p$ subgroup of $H$. By Sylow's Theorem, there exists a $p$ subgroup $P$ of $G$, and there exists some $g\in G$ such that $Q \leq gPg^{-1}$. Since $Q \subset gPg^{-1} \cap H$, we have that $|gPg^{-1} \cap H| \geq |Q| = p^\beta$. But by lagranges theorem we also have that $p^beta \big| |gPg^{-1}\cap H|$, so $p^\beta \leq |gPg^{-1} \cap H|$. This proves the result.
