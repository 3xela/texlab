\begin{document} \noindent Q2: Let $p(x) = x^3+x+1$. If $p$ is reducible, it must split into a factors of degree $2$ and $1$. 
By prop.11 of section 9.4 (Dummite and Foote), $p$ will be reducible if $p(-1)$ or $p(1) $ are 0. We see that clearly neither are $0$. 
Thus $p(x)$ is irreducible over $\Q$.
